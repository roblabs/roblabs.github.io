* Link to the full screen map: [3LineMaps.com](https://3linemaps.com/50.html)

## Colophon

### Architecture & Design Choices

The map was based on Apple Maps, interactive maps on the Web, which offers standard and satellite maps types.  While Apple Maps will work on most browsers for desktop and mobile, the customer may want to view the landmark in their favorite map app. The landmark callouts were designed with a *mobile first* experience in mind.  Making a map also should take into consideration a customers privacy<sup>1</sup>.  

Tapping on a hike location ![image](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/118112/120080190-909ad800-c06c-11eb-8749-ad1f61351054.png) brings up the landmark callout.  From there the customer can choose a link to open a map app that offers features they expect: directions, map printing, saving & sharing of landmarks via text or social media, or possibly offline map downloads.  Customers viewing this map on Mac, iPhone or Android can enjoy the proper map app for their system.

## Cartography

* The landmark callout include the distance, elevation and comments as provided by the Sierra Club.
  * The layout is handled by Apple Maps, with formatting of HTML elements such as headers and links.
* The font for the landmark callouts is [Montserrat](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Montserrat) which is also one of the 50 hikes called [Monserate Mountain Trail](https://maps.apple.com/?auid=1945840914501037067).
* The color palette for the landmark callouts also comes from the North County Group
  * ![0x00bfff](https://img.shields.io/badge/-0x00bfff-00bfff) `#00bfff`, RGB = (0, 191, 255), *DeepSkyBlue*
